Early Assessment Program (EAP)

 

Fullerton College is now accepting test scores for the English and Math components of the EAP examination.

Qualifying students will be exempted from additional placement testing in English and/or *Math (see exception below) at Fullerton College. To qualify for an English and/or Math placement exemption, you must:

  • complete the EAP English and Math components as a part of your 11th grade STAR Testing,
  • receive
    • an "English Status" that states, "Ready for CSU or participating CCC college-level English courses", and/or
    • a "Math Status" that states, "Ready for CSU or participating CCC college-level mathematics courses" OR "Ready for CSU or participating CCC college-level mathematics courses - Conditional"
  • submit the appropriate documentation to Fullerton College, please see below for more detailed information about your EAP status and petition requirements.

 

Exempted students will not need to take the Fullerton College English and/or *Math assessment test, however, students may still need to take the reading assessment (and chemistry assessment - if planning to take CHEM 111AF).

*Exception: students who have completed and passed advanced math courses in high school (e.g. trig, pre-calculus, calculus, etc.) and who intend to pursue a major that would require advanced, college-level math may still need to take the Fullerton College math assessment test in order to be placed directly into Math 150A (Calculus) - it is recommended that these students meet with a Fullerton College Counselor as soon as possible to discuss the most appropriate course of action.

For additional information about the EAP, please visit:

  • Ready for CSU or participating CCC college-level mathematics courses
  • Ready for CSU or participating CCC college-level mathematics courses – Conditional

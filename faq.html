Questions & Answers

 

How Do I see a Counselor?
For information on seeing a counselor,  see the Counseling Appointments/ Online Counseling.  For a list of counselors and their assigned areas see the Staff .
Will I be assigned a counselor?
Now that you are a college student, you are not assigned a counselor. You can decide which counselor you want to work with most and request that counselor when you make an appointment. Majors are assigned to counseling teams, so you may want to inquire which counselors are most familiar with your major.
Do I need my high school or other college transcripts to see a counselor?
The more information you can provide the counselor with, the more valuable the counseling session will be.  Other college transcripts are very important to insure that you are taking the right courses at Fullerton.
Do I have to take the placement tests, if I want to take a class that has no prerequisites?
 Placement tests are highly recommended for all students. However, you may enroll in a class that has no prerequisites without the placement tests.
Do I have to declare a major? What if I am undecided about my goal?
It is not necessary to declare a major during your first semester. However, you are expected to spend time exploring your educational options. We recommend visiting the Career and Life Planning Center or your counselor to explore educational options
What should I do, if I receive a D, F or NCR grade?
It depends on your course of study and your educational goal. Please see a counselor to discuss your options as you have several choices, which include the exclusion of substandard grades.
Do I have to see a counselor to register for classes?
No, you are not required to see a counselor prior to your registration. However, if you are planning to register for an English and/or Math class, you must have your prerequisites checked and approved first. Counselors do not register students or sign student programs.
Do Fullerton College courses transfer to California State Universities or University of California?

All Fullerton College courses with course numbers 100 or above do transfer to the California State University.

For transfer to the University of California, please check the transfer designation under the course listing in the college catalog.  If the course is UC transferable there will be a note to this effect.

Also, you may visit the ASSIST website at www.assist.org to find out transferability of courses.

If I get an AA/AS degree from Fullerton College, can I automatically transfer to a university?
No. Admissions to a university are based on different criteria. Please see a counselor for transfer requirements. By working with a counselor when you begin college, you can achieve both of these educational options.
How do I find out about the programs, majors, degrees and certificates offered at Fullerton College?
Consult the college catalog and/or see a counselor.
Can I get financial aid?
About 40% of FC students receive some sort of financial aid. There are fee waivers, grants, scholarships, work-study and loans available. Students are encouraged to apply. Contact the Financial Aid Office in room 115.
